Lesson 8: Ordering Food at a Restaurant

In this lesson, you'll learn essential phrases for ordering food in Spanish at a restaurant. You'll practice listening to common waiter-customer interactions and learn food-related vocabulary.

Listen to the Dialogue

Transcript

Waiter: Buenas tardes, ¿qué desean ordenar?

Customer: Quisiera la paella, por favor.

Waiter: ¿Algo para beber?

Customer: Un vaso de vino tinto, gracias.

Watch the Video Lesson

Key Vocabulary

  • La carta - The menu
  • Ordenar - To order
  • El plato principal - Main course
  • La cuenta - The bill

Grammar Focus

In this lesson we use the conditional tense "Quisiera" (I would like) which is more polite than "Quiero" (I want) when ordering food.
